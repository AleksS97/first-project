Prologomena is an imaginary restaurant and the brainchild of Aleksandra Skica, a Brooklyn College computer science student who made this project as her first website. She developed a love for cooking when she decided to become a vegetarian as a child and had to prepare many of her own meals. Over the years, her culinary tastes evolved and although she is no longer a vegetarian, her dishes are mainly plant-based and contain only chicken and seafood. The dishes at Prologomena are crafted with attention to ingredients, texture, taste, color, and presentation. Aleksandra takes a holistic look at cooking and regards it as an art and a science. She is a big believer in fats, which she uses liberally in her cooking. Menu highlights include lots of avocado and artisinal cheeses from throughout the world.

The restaurant's name is inspired by the title of Prologomena to Any Future Metaphysics by Immanuel Kant, a thinker our chef was reading while working on this project and who she she does not enjoy reading.
